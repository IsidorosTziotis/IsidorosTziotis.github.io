About Me

I am currently a Ph.D. student in the ECE Department (DICE track), at the University of Texas at Austin, where I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Aryan Mokhtari. Furthermore, I am thrilled to be a member of the amazing Wireless Networking and Communications Group (WNCG), an interdisciplinary center for research and education located at UT Austin. Before joining UT Austin I completed my undergraduate studies at the Department of Informatics and Telecommunications in Greece and received my MSc in Logic, Algorithms and Theory of Computation from the Department of Mathematics at the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens under the supervision of Prof. Dimitris Fotakis.

My research interests revolve around the theory and applications of optimization in large-scale machine learning problems. More precisely, my focus lies on various aspects of Federated Learning and Decentralized Optimization. In addition to that, I find the area of Adaptive Optimization extremely interesting with my most recent work taking aim at deriving analysis that bridges the gap between SGD and adaptive methods in a broad class of regimes.

I worked as an applied scientist intern in the group of Alexa at Amazon where the focus of my work lied on developing and analyzing scalable semi-asynchronous Federated Learning algorithms with robust performance in the presence of stragglers and data-heterogeneous clients in the network (data & system heterogeneity). Further, our experimental results showcased the superior performance of our straggler-resilient methods over established baselines in academic (CIFAR10, CIFAR100, FEMNIST) and industry datasets.
